What Does an Employment Lawyer Do?

An employment lawyer specializes in various aspects of employment law, including:

  • Wrongful Dismissal: Representing clients who have been terminated without just cause.
  • Employment Contracts: Reviewing and negotiating contracts to ensure fair terms.
  • Workplace Harassment: Assisting victims of discrimination or harassment in filing complaints.
  • Severance Packages: Helping clients secure fair severance pay when they lose their job.

Why You Need an Employment Lawyer

Common Employment Issues in Edmonton

Understanding common employment issues can help you identify when to seek legal assistance:

  • Wrongful Termination: If you feel you were fired unfairly, consulting a lawyer can clarify your rights.
  • Discrimination: If you face discrimination based on race, gender, or disability, legal support is crucial.
  • Unpaid Wages: If your employer fails to pay wages owed, an employment lawyer can help recover those funds.

How to Choose the Right Employment Lawyer

Selecting the right employment lawyer is critical for your case. Here are some tips:

  • Experience: Look for a lawyer with a proven track record in employment law.
  • Specialization: Ensure they specialize in the specific area of employment law relevant to your case.
  • Client Reviews: Check testimonials or reviews from previous clients to gauge their effectiveness.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What should I do if I think I was wrongfully terminated?

If you believe you were wrongfully terminated, document all relevant details and consult an employment lawyer as soon as possible.

2. How much does it cost to hire an employment lawyer in Edmonton?

Costs vary depending on the complexity of your case and the fee structure of the lawyer. Many offer flexible arrangements like contingency fees or hourly rates.

3. Can I represent myself in an employment dispute?

While it's possible to represent yourself, having an experienced lawyer significantly increases your chances of success.

4. What are my rights regarding workplace harassment?

Employees have the right to work in an environment free from harassment. If you experience harassment, consult a lawyer to explore your options.
